What is the CCCBA PRO?CCCBA PRO is the Contra Costa County Bar Association Professional Referral Organization.PRO is the Bar Association’s business referralnetworking group.

How do I join?Joining PRO is simple. If you are a CCCBA member or affiliate member, you are already eligibleto apply. If you are not a member, now is a greattime to join the CCCBA and you will then be provided with a separate PRO application.

Can anyone join?Yes, and no. While PRO is open to all CCCBAmembers, both attorneys and non-attorneys, thePRO group is limited to two to three participantsin any particular category who have at least 7years’ experience. So, if you are a family law attorney, you and only one or two others may bein your PRO group, one to three financial planners,CPAs, etc. The group can define classifications to narrow them with the participant’s approvalso that there could be sub-specializations allowing more family law attorneys in a group. As wehave more requests from similar practice areas,we will expand to more groups.We currently have one group based in WalnutCreek that meets virtually. We aim to meet inperson once per quarter.The PRO management committee will reviewmembership applications for approval. Solong as the member is in good standing withall professional boards (Bar, CPA, DRE, etc.)and has no history of actions against their professional license(s), the member is eligible

to participate and their application will be

forwarded to the groups who will then decide

whether to admit the individual.

What does it cost?The cost to become a PRO member is $300 inaddition to your CCCBA membership fee.

Why join?CCCBA’s mission statement is to “Empower ourmembers to deliver outstanding legal service to our community” and our vision is that “TheCCCBA empowers our members to build thriving legal practices that deliver outstanding legalservice to our community. We do this througheducating our members, fostering fellowshipamong members of the bar, building strongrelationships with the court and connecting thecommunity with the legal assistance it needs.”The CCCBA feels that networking referralgroups further the CCCBA’s mission and are oftremendous benefit to our members to assistthem in building their practices and better serving their communities. We purposely set theprice at $300 to eliminate the barrier of cost toparticipation. The goal is to exchange referrals,and also to create a stronger sense of community within the group.

How will the group run?CCCBA PRO meets monthly via Zoom for 90minutes, and has a facilitator to run the group.CCCBA PRO will review applications for conflicts and to ensure a proper fit for the applicant. What happens during a meeting?The members of the group will all have a chanceto introduce themselves and their practice during each meeting. Each meeting will includelonger presentations from members so thatmembers have an opportunity to learn abouteach other from more than just a brief introduction. During each meeting, members will alsohave the opportunity to thank other membersfor referrals received. Typically, the PRO meetings will NOT include MCLE presentations.

What obligations will a PRO member have?Members of PRO will be expected to attendeach monthly meeting and participate in thediscussions. We understand that life happens,but continual lack of attendance will result indismissal from PRO without a refund. There is norequirement on the number of referrals given/received, but hopefully members will considertheir fellow PRO members when giving referrals in appropriate matters. Members will alsobe expected to have one or two meetings permonth with other members of their respectivePRO group outside of the regular meeting. Thegroup will track attendance, outside meetings,and referrals within their group and will considerthese statistics upon annual renewal requests.Failure to actively participate can result in dismissal from the group or the group not allowinga participant’s renewal. We want actively engaged members.For questions or more information, please contact Jody Iorns at jiorns@cccba.org.
